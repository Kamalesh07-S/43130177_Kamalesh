DOSA

A South Indian Delight Dosa is a popular South Indian dish that is enjoyed all over India and even globally. It is a thin, crispy pancake made from a fermented batter of rice and urad dal (black gram). Dosas are typically served with chutneys (coconut, tomato, or mint) and sambar (a flavorful lentil soup with vegetables). Types of Dosa There are various types of dosa, each with its own unique flavor and texture: Plain Dosa – A simple, crispy dosa without any filling. Masala Dosa – A dosa filled with a spiced potato mixture. Rava Dosa – Made with semolina (rava), it has a lacy, crispy texture. Set Dosa – Thick and soft dosas, usually served in sets of two or three. Mysore Masala Dosa – A spicier version of masala dosa with red chutney spread inside. Neer Dosa – A soft, thin dosa made from rice batter without fermentation. Pesarattu – A dosa made with green gram (moong dal), popular in Andhra Pradesh.

IDLI

Idli: A Soft and Healthy South Indian Delicacy Idli is a traditional South Indian dish made from a fermented batter of rice and urad dal (black gram). It is steamed to perfection, resulting in soft, fluffy, and nutritious rice cakes. Idlis are a staple breakfast item in India and are loved for their light texture and easy digestibility. Health Benefits of Idli Easy to digest – Fermentation makes it gut-friendly. Rich in carbohydrates and protein – Provides sustained energy. Gluten-free – Suitable for people with gluten intolerance. Low in calories – A great option for weight watchers.
